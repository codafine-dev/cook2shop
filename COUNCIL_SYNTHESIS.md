# COUNCIL SYNTHESIS: Cook2shop
## 1. The Debate Transcript

**Nexus (HW/IoT):** "The bridge from 'Recipe' to 'Cart' needs to be frictionless. We need a robust mapping engine that handles brand substitutions and local store availability."

**Lumina (Psych):** "The friction is in the 'Decision Fatigue'. The AI shouldn't just add items; it should suggest 'Simplified Versions' of the recipe based on the user's skill level."

**Sora (UX):** "The PWA must feel like a kitchen tool, not an e-commerce app. Minimalist, one-handed operation, voice-enabled for when the hands are covered in flour."

**Atlas (Compliance):** "GDPR and payment security are standard, but the 'Substitution Logic' must be transparent to avoid user frustration with incorrect orders."

**Nova (Vision):** "The future is 'Intent-Based Shopping'. The AI knows you want a 'Healthy Italian Dinner' and auto-populates the cart based on your dietary preferences and the recipe."

**Kael (Strategist):** "The arbitrage is in the 'Affiliate conversion'. By owning the 'Intent' (the recipe), we control the 'Conversion' (the cart)."

**Common (Sane):** "Let's not overengineer the AI. A good regex and a mapping table solve 80% of the problems. Don't use a LLM for simple ingredient matching."

**Mercator (Value):** "The value proposition is 'Time Saved'. From 'I want to cook this' to 'Ingredients are arriving' in 3 clicks."

## 2. Final Consensus Conclusion
- **Logic:** Recipe $\rightarrow$ Ingredient Extraction $\rightarrow$ Store Mapping $\rightarrow$ Cart.
- **UX:** PWA-first, voice-integrated, focus on reducing decision fatigue.
- **Business:** Affiliate-driven model leveraging 'Intent' capture.
- **Technical:** Lean mapping engine with LLM-assisted substitution only for complex items.
